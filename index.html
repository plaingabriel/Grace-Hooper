Grace Murray Hopper

The woman who revolutiozined the computer industry

Grace Hooper and her team working on a computer

Here's a time line of Grace Hooper:

  • 1906 - Born in New York City, New York.
  • 1925 - Begins school at Vassar College in New York - studying mathematics and physics.
  • 1928 - Graduates from Vassar and moves on to study at Yale University, working on a Master's degree in Mathematics.
  • 1930 - Gets a Master's degree in Mathematics. Also marries Vincent Foster Hopper and becomes Grace Hopper, a name will keep long after their divorce in 1945.
  • 1931 - Becomes a teacher at Vassar, where used to go, and also keeps studying at Yale, hoping to get a Ph.D. in Mathematics.
  • 1934 - Gets her Ph.D. in Mathematics from Yale, becoming the first woman to earn such a degree.
  • 1943 - World War II compels her into joining the US Naval Reserve. Opts for the Navy, as it was her grandfather's branch of service.
  • 1944 - Is commissioned as lieutenant in 1944, and given her mathematical background, is assigned to the Bureau of Ordnance Project at Harvard, where learns to program a Mark I computer.
  • 1952 - Her team creates the first compiler for computer languages (a compiler renders worded instructions into code that can be read by computers).
  • 1966 - Retires from the Naval Reserve, but her pioneering computer work means that is recalled for active duty — at the age of 60 — to tackle standardizing communication between different computer languages.
  • 1986 - Retires at age 79, being a rear admiral as well as the oldest serving officer in the service.
  • 1987 - Saying that she would be "bored stiff" if she stopped working entirely, takes another job post-retirement and stayes in the computer industry for several more years.
  • 1991 - Is awarded the National Medal of Technology, becoming the first female individual recipient of the honor.
  • 1992 - Dies at the age of 85.
  • 2016 - Is posthumously honored with the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Barack Obama.
